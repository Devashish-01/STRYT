# STRYT — Grievance Redressal Policy

**Effective Date:** [To be set by STRYT on publication]
**Last Updated:** 19 July 2026
**Version:** 1.0 (draft for legal review)

STRYT is committed to addressing your concerns fairly and promptly. This Grievance Redressal Policy explains how to raise a grievance and how we handle it. It is published in accordance with the **Information Technology Act, 2000** and the **Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021** ("Intermediary Guidelines"), the **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023** ("DPDP Act"), and the **Consumer Protection Act, 2019** and its e-commerce rules, as applicable. It forms part of the [Terms & Conditions](terms-and-conditions.md).

---

## 1. What this covers

Use this process to raise, among other things:
- content you believe violates the [Acceptable Use Policy](acceptable-use-policy.md) or [Community Guidelines](community-guidelines.md) (e.g. unlawful, infringing, harassing, or harmful content);
- privacy/data grievances and requests under the DPDP Act (access, correction, erasure, consent withdrawal, nomination — see the [Privacy Policy](privacy-policy.md));
- complaints about another User, a Seller, a listing, a transaction, or a payment record;
- reports of impersonation, fraud, safety concerns, or misuse of safety features; and
- intellectual-property (copyright/trademark) complaints.

For general how-to help, use **Account → Help & Support** in the app.

## 3. How to file a grievance

Provide as much of the following as possible so we can act efficiently:
1. your name and contact details (and your STRYT handle/alias, if any);
2. a clear description of the grievance;
3. the specific content, User, listing, transaction, or data involved (with links/IDs/screenshots where possible — e.g. the profile, request, post, review, or the booking/deal/queue/walk-in and any UPI reference);
4. what outcome you are seeking; and
5. for IP complaints, proof of your rights and a good-faith statement; for data-rights requests, enough information for us to verify your identity.

Submit via the in-app **Report** option (on a profile/listing/request/post/review), **Account → Help & Support**, or by email/post to the Grievance Officer above.

## 4. Timelines

Consistent with the Intermediary Guidelines and applicable law, STRYT will:
- **Acknowledge** your grievance within **24 hours** of receipt;
- **Resolve** it within **15 days** of receipt (and sooner where the law requires — for example, requests to remove certain non-consensual intimate or impersonation content are actioned on an expedited basis, within the shorter statutory timeline);
- for **data-protection requests** under the DPDP Act, respond within the timelines required by that Act and its rules.

If a matter needs more time or third-party input (e.g. the other party's response, or law-enforcement involvement), we will keep you reasonably informed.

## 5. How we handle grievances

5.1 We review each grievance fairly, may contact you or other involved parties for more information, and take proportionate action — which may include no action (if unfounded), content removal or restriction, warnings, feature limits, verification review, suspension, or termination, and referral to authorities where appropriate.

5.2 As an intermediary, STRYT does not adjudicate the underlying commercial merits of a Seller–Customer dispute (e.g. product quality) as a court would; for such matters we may moderate, provide records we hold, and facilitate resolution, but your primary remedy for goods/services is against the Seller (see the [Refund & Cancellation Policy](refund-cancellation-policy.md)).

5.3 We may decline or limit action on grievances that are unlawful, abusive, repetitive/vexatious, or outside our control, and we will not remove lawful content merely because it is unflattering.

5.4 Actions taken are recorded (e.g. in audit logs) for accountability.

## 6. Escalation

6.1 If you are dissatisfied with the outcome, you may reply to the Grievance Officer requesting escalation/review.

6.2 **Data-protection matters:** you may complain to the **Data Protection Board of India** as provided under the DPDP Act.

6.3 **Consumer matters:** you retain your rights under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, including approaching the appropriate **Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission** and, for online purchases, the **National Consumer Helpline**. (Your claim for defective goods/services generally lies against the Seller.)

6.4 **Intermediary Guidelines:** where applicable, you may pursue remedies before the relevant Grievance Appellate Committee or authority constituted under the Intermediary Guidelines.

6.5 Nothing here limits your right to seek legal remedies, subject to the governing-law, jurisdiction, and arbitration terms in the [Terms & Conditions](terms-and-conditions.md).
