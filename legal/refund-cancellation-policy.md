This Refund & Cancellation Policy forms part of the [Terms & Conditions](terms-and-conditions.md) and must be read with them, the [Merchant & Provider Terms](merchant-terms.md), and the [Grievance Redressal Policy](grievance-redressal-policy.md).

> **The single most important point:** STRYT does not hold, process, or control your money. Payments happen **directly between you and the shop or provider** (by UPI or cash). Because STRYT never receives your funds, **STRYT cannot issue refunds**. This Policy explains how refunds and cancellations actually work on the Platform and what each party is responsible for.

---

## 1. STRYT's role in payments and refunds

1.1 STRYT is a discovery and coordination platform, **not** a seller, payment aggregator, payment gateway, escrow agent, or bank (see Terms Section 13).

1.2 When you pay for a booking, deal, queue visit, or walk-in purchase, you pay the **Seller directly** via a UPI deep-link/QR that the app generates, or by cash. The money goes from your bank/UPI app to the Seller's account (or hand-to-hand for cash) and never passes through STRYT.

1.3 The app's **"I have paid" / "confirm" / "reject"** buttons and any **"held" / "released"** labels are **status markers for the parties' convenience** — they are not a STRYT-operated escrow and do not move, hold, or return money.

1.4 **Consequently, all refunds are between you and the Seller.** STRYT has no ability to reverse a UPI transfer or return cash, and does not do so.

## 2. Refunds from Sellers (the actual refund path)

2.1 If you are entitled to a refund — for example the goods/service were not delivered, were defective, or the Seller agrees to cancel — the **Seller** is responsible for refunding you directly, by the same UPI route or cash, according to the Seller's own refund practice and applicable law (including the Consumer Protection Act, 2019).

2.2 Each Seller sets and is responsible for its own refund, return, and cancellation practices. Before paying, check the Seller's stated terms and, where in doubt, confirm with the Seller in chat.

2.3 If a Seller refuses a refund you believe you are owed, you may:
- raise a **dispute** on the Deal/Agreement (where the transaction was a Deal), which a STRYT Admin may review and use to update the Deal's **status** (mark complete or cancel) — noting this only changes the in-app status and does **not** move money;
- use the [Grievance Redressal Policy](grievance-redressal-policy.md) to bring it to STRYT's attention; and/or
- pursue your independent legal/consumer remedies directly against the Seller.

2.4 STRYT may, at its discretion, assist by moderating, providing transaction records it holds, or acting on reports, but STRYT is **not obliged to** and **cannot** compensate you for a Seller's refund.

## 3. Cancellations

### 3.1 Appointments / bookings
- A Customer may cancel or request to reschedule an upcoming appointment from **My Appointments**; a Seller may accept or decline the change.
- A Seller may decline a booking request, or cancel a confirmed booking with a reason, and may mark no-shows.
- If you paid in advance for an appointment that is cancelled, any refund is handled **by the Seller directly** per Section 2.
- Repeated cancellations or no-shows may affect your reputation on the Platform.

### 3.2 Deals / Agreements (from accepted quotes)
- A Deal is created when a Customer accepts a quote and must be confirmed within a short window (about 10 minutes) or it expires.
- Either party may cancel a Deal before payment/performance, per the in-app flow. Mid-job problems can be raised as a **dispute** with a written reason.
- An Admin resolving a dispute updates the Deal's **status only** (release/complete or cancel). Any money already exchanged directly between the parties is **not** affected by that status change and must be settled between the parties.

### 3.3 Live queue slots
- You may leave a queue at any time. Once **called** or being served, leaving may require confirming you'll lose your place.
- Joining or holding a queue slot does not involve a STRYT-held payment; any payment for a queue visit is a direct Customer–Business payment and any refund is per Section 2.

### 3.4 Walk-in purchases
- Walk-in payments are recorded after a direct, in-person transaction. Refunds/returns for walk-in purchases are handled **by the Business directly** per its own practice and applicable law.

## 4. Failed, wrong, or disputed payments

4.1 **Wrong recipient / wrong amount.** Because you initiate UPI payments in your own UPI app, you are responsible for confirming the payee and amount before paying. STRYT cannot recover a payment sent to the wrong person; contact your bank/UPI provider and the recipient.

4.2 **Payment made but not received / not confirmed.** If you paid but the Seller hasn't confirmed, first message the Seller. A UPI reference (from your UPI app) is your primary proof of a UPI transfer — STRYT does not store or verify it. Unresolved cases can go through Grievance Redressal.

4.3 **"Paid" claimed but not actually paid.** Sellers should confirm receipt only after verifying it in their own bank/UPI records, and may **reject** a false claim. Falsely claiming or confirming payment is a breach of the Terms and Acceptable Use Policy.

## 6. Consumer rights preserved

6.1 Nothing in this Policy limits any non-waivable right you have under the **Consumer Protection Act, 2019** or other applicable law, including the right to approach consumer dispute redressal forums against a Seller for deficient goods/services.

6.2 STRYT, as an intermediary that does not sell the goods/services or hold funds, is generally not the correct respondent for a product/service refund; your primary recourse is the Seller. STRYT will provide reasonable cooperation and any records it holds as required by law.
