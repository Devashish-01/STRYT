# Quotes & Deals — From Offer to Agreement

**Getting quotes.** Once you post a request, nearby shops, providers, and other customers can send back a priced **quote (proposal)** with a pitch and timing. You'll see every quote on your request's detail page.

**Negotiating.** You can counter-offer a quote's price, message the responder directly, or just accept it outright — the full back-and-forth is visible to both of you, so there's no confusion about what was actually agreed.

**Accepting creates a Deal.** The moment you accept a quote, it becomes a tracked **Deal** (technically an "Agreement") between you and that one responder. Both sides get a **10-minute window** to confirm before it automatically expires — so don't sit on it.

**What a Deal looks like.** A 5-stage progress bar (Confirmed → Deposit → In progress → Review → Done) plus plain-language "what happens next" text tailored to whichever side you're on — see the **"Tracking a Deal"** guide for the live-status and payment side of this.

**If something goes wrong.** Either side can cancel before payment moves. If a real problem comes up mid-job, either of you can raise a **dispute** with a written reason — STRYT's team reviews it and resolves it by releasing or cancelling the (never-actually-held-by-STRYT) payment status.

**After it's done.** You're prompted to leave a 1–5 star rating with quick tags (On time, Great quality, Fair price…), a comment, and an optional cash tip — this is what builds the local reputation system that makes future quotes more trustworthy.
