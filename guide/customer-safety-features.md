# Safety Features — "My People" & Location Sharing

STRYT includes real personal-safety tools for meeting people from the app in person.

**Live location sharing with "My People."** Instantly share your continuously-updating live location with your chosen emergency contacts — delivered as a live map inside your chat with each of them, until you manually stop sharing. Find this via the persistent safety icon/banner, or the **Safety Hub**.

**Emergency contacts.** You choose who your live-location shares can go to — and STRYT limits this to people you've already chatted with in the app, as a built-in trust boundary (never a total stranger). Manage this list in **Safety Hub → Emergency contacts**.

**Requesting someone's exact location.** On any user's public profile, you can ask them to reveal their exact location just once. They have to **approve** before it's shown to you, and the share is **time-limited and revocable** — you can't just take someone's exact location without their say-so. Manage approvals for requests made *to you* in **Settings**.

**Why exact location is private by default.** STRYT never stores your location history — only your last known spot — and your exact coordinates aren't shared with strangers by default. This balances genuinely needing to meet up with the fact that your precise location is sensitive.

**Use good judgement.** These tools reduce risk, but treat meeting anyone from the app the way you would any stranger — meet in a public spot when you can, and share your plans with someone you trust.
