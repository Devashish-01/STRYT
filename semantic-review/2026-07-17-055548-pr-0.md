# Responsive customer account hub

The proposed direction replaces `Profile.tsx`‚Äôs separately maintained mobile and desktop pages with one customer-first hub composed responsively in CSS. It should preserve the ambient identity surface, stats, queues and deals, highlights, customer destinations, account switching, and settings while moving seller management out of the primary hierarchy. `AccountSwitcher` is the right replacement for coarse `activeRole` tiles because it selects concrete owned and delegated accounts, but the surrounding state and loading contracts are not ready to make it the sole control. **Watch for:** **confirmed** stale `activeRole` consumers after switching moves to `activeContext`; **confirmed** accounts briefly disappearing while `AccountSwitcher` loads; **confirmed** false zero/empty customer state during fetches; and **confirmed** desktop losing active deals and queue detail if its current behavior becomes the consolidation baseline.

## High-level view

Render each behavioral unit once: one identity hero, stats strip, `Right now` collection, highlights reel, customer-action collection, and account/settings tail. **Confirmed:** the current trees have diverged behaviorally, so renamed mobile/desktop branches would preserve the underlying problem. Responsive account-hub classes should consume the existing spacing, radius, typography, surface, and semantic-color tokens.

The ambient hero should contain identity and utilities rather than precede a second desktop identity card. **Confirmed:** both branches invent `@username` when no alias exists and expose different privacy/public-profile controls. Conditionally show a real alias and preserve the privacy state, neighbour preview, customer-scoped inbox badges, edit, and share.

`AccountSwitcher` should be the only selector on this page; seller dashboards and onboarding are secondary destinations. **Confirmed:** its model handles concrete owned and delegated accounts, but its queries begin when the sheet mounts and have no loading presentation. **Confirmed:** `Support.tsx` still initializes ticket identity from `activeRole`, so the migration can attribute support reports to the wrong role.

The shared `Right now` block should use the richer mobile contract: individual queue position/wait details and every active agreement. **Confirmed:** desktop currently aggregates queues and omits agreements, while agreements are fetched once even though queues subscribe to realtime changes. Time-sensitive content belongs above highlights and static actions, with loading/error states instead of transient zeroes or ‚Äúnone.‚Äù

The Playwright audit visits `/profile` at phone and desktop sizes, but **confirmed** it does not assert content parity, account completeness, action destinations, missing-alias rendering, loading/error behavior, or live-state updates.

## One account-hub tree, two responsive compositions

`Profile.tsx` starts a complete mobile page at line 146 and later renders a second desktop page. **Confirmed:** the duplication has produced different privacy controls and live content, not just different layouts. Keep the shared DOM in this customer-priority order and let desktop CSS compose it into columns without creating alternate controls:

```text
identity hero
    ‚Üì
stats
    ‚Üì
right now (queues + deals)
    ‚Üì
highlights
    ‚Üì
customer activity / identity destinations
    ‚Üì
account switching ¬∑ settings ¬∑ seller management
```

One stats model and one action-tile model should preserve the existing routes: Saved ‚Üí `/bookmarks`, Following ‚Üí `/bookmarks?tab=following`, Followers ‚Üí `/followers`, Deals ‚Üí `/agreements`, plus appointments, requests, queues, achievements, community, activity, lists, and public profile.

**Confirmed:** collapsing the JSX alone would not make the screen token-driven. Profile currently hardcodes white fills, translucent colors, pixel radii, spacing, shadows, and type sizes throughout. Move presentation into account-hub classes in `src/index.css`; use `--surface`, `--line`, `--space-*`, `--radius-*`, the semantic type scale, and color ramps, adding a token before any missing ambient/translucent value. Pass the data-driven ambient gradient through a CSS custom property rather than retaining inline layout styling.

## Customer identity remains functional inside the ambient hero

The mobile hero currently owns avatar/name, location and alias, customer chat and notification actions, privacy state, neighbour preview, edit, and share. Desktop splits those responsibilities between a generic ambient title and a white identity card, replacing the actionable privacy row with a badge. Use the mobile behavior as the shared contract and adapt only its composition at desktop widths.

**Confirmed:** `Profile.tsx:176` and `Profile.tsx:487` render the literal fallback `@username`. Render `@alias ¬∑ area` only when `user.alias` exists; otherwise render the area without an orphan separator. Do not synthesize a handle from a name, phone, or user id. Preserve `displayName(user.name)`, the `showNamePublicly` cue, and `/u/:id` preview.

Messages must continue to open `/chats?scope=CUSTOMER`, notifications `/notifications?scope=CUSTOMER`, and both unread indicators must remain customer-scoped. Edit must retain `/profile/edit`; share must retain eligible personal/business/provider options; and avatar/name/privacy actions must retain `/u/${user.id}`.

## AccountSwitcher becomes the context boundary

The current tiles model three coarse roles, use `ownedBusinessIds[0]` for every business owner, ignore delegated businesses, and call `setActiveRole` only for Customer (`Profile.tsx:347-355` and the desktop duplicate). **Confirmed:** they can disagree with the `activeContext` driving navigation and cannot select among multiple businesses. `useAccountOptions()` instead creates options for every owned business, provider profile, and active delegated grant; `pick()` updates `activeContext` and navigates to that account.

**Confirmed:** `Support.tsx:17-27` still derives its initial `reporterRole` from `activeRole`, while `useAccountOptions().pick()` changes only `activeContext`. Migrate Support to `activeContext.type`, or synchronize the legacy field inside `setContext` while retiring it; do not keep independently persisted account selectors.

**Confirmed:** making the sheet the sole selector can initially claim accounts do not exist. `AccountSwitcher` mounts `useAccountOptions` only after opening, then starts business, provider, and delegated-session queries while immediately rendering a Customer-only option list with no loading/error state. Expose query status and render option skeletons/retry, or preload account options in a mounted shared control. Do not offer ‚ÄúBecome a provider‚Äù or imply Customer is the only account until discovery settles.

**Confirmed:** gating the trigger with `hasSellerProfile = ownedBusinessIds.length > 0 || !!ownedProviderId` excludes delegated-only access. Base visibility on the canonical option model. Keep management and onboarding inside the switcher or a quiet tail section rather than recreating seller tiles beside customer identity.

## Right now uses the richer mobile contract

Mobile renders every waiting/called queue with business, position or ‚ÄúYour turn,‚Äù estimated wait, and queue action, followed by every non-terminal agreement and its direct route (`Profile.tsx:280-308`). Desktop renders one aggregate queue banner and no agreements. **Confirmed:** taking desktop as the baseline would remove deal visibility and queue detail. Render one shared live-row collection at every width; desktop may change columns, not information.

**Confirmed:** the block is only partially live. Queues use `useQueryWithRealtime(..., "queue_tokens")`; agreements use `useQuery(requestService.agreements)` at `Profile.tsx:77`. Subscribe to agreement changes or refetch on lifecycle events so an open hub does not retain a stale deal state.

**Confirmed:** mobile currently places highlights before `Right now`, giving archived identity content priority over a called queue or pending confirmation. Keep stats under identity, then queues/deals, then highlights and static destinations.

## Truthful customer summaries while data settles

The screen computes followers, agreements, appointments, requests, queues, and highlights from `data ?? []`. **Confirmed:** loading and failed queries therefore render `0`, ‚ÄúNone upcoming,‚Äù ‚ÄúNone open,‚Äù ‚ÄúNot in line,‚Äù or no section. `useQuery` already exposes `loading`, `error`, and `refetch`; reserve zero/none for successful empty responses and show neutral loading or retry states otherwise.

Preserve the last successful value during realtime refetches rather than flashing populated sections back to empty. Apply the same rule to AccountSwitcher options, where false absence changes what the customer believes they can access.

## Customer-first tail without burying account access

The desktop role card sits beside identity, and the mobile seller card precedes settings. **Confirmed:** both make selling a primary purpose of a customer hub. Place seller management/onboarding after customer content, style it as tertiary, and remove a standalone promotion when `AccountSwitcher` already exposes the action.

Account switching is account infrastructure, not a seller promotion, so keep a clearly labeled trigger discoverable near the identity/account boundary. Keep `/account` stable and do not make settings subordinate to ‚ÄúStart selling‚Äù or duplicate settings in both the action grid and footer.

## Regression coverage beyond route screenshots

`tests/routes.ts` includes `/profile`, and the audit runs it in Pixel 7 and desktop Chrome. It catches blank output, crashes, console/server failures, mobile overflow, and records screenshots.

**Confirmed:** no targeted test asserts equal sections and destinations at both widths, conditional alias punctuation, live-deal visibility, or eventual owned/delegated AccountSwitcher options. Add focused coverage for those contracts plus loading, empty, and failed queries. Include desktop keyboard order and mobile safe-area/bottom-nav behavior because one scroll container will replace two implementations.
